In mainland China, “One China, two systems” (一国两制) has been drilled into my head in the few years I lived there as a kid--Hong Kong is but another city in the country, just with a different political system; July 1st every year is celebrated as the anniversary of Hong Kong being returned home to China from the British rule in 1997.

Some older Hong Kong residents may still cite Hong Kong as part of China--many of them emigrated from China, after all; but the younger Hong Kong generation will disagree. Hong Kong as they see it, is not only separate from China, but superior: they do not live under the communist regime, they have more civil rights--and just a better quality of life in general.

And Cantonese becomes the ultimate symbol of this superiority.
